This project analyzes Formula 1 pit stop performance using statistical methods to understand how pit stop times vary across teams and over different racing seasons. Pit stops are a critical factor in race strategy, where even small time differences can impact overall race outcomes.
The goal of this analysis is to answer the research question:

Do pit stop times differ by team and year, and is there an interaction between team and year?

To address this, a two-way ANOVA model was used to evaluate:

Differences in mean pit stop times across teams
Differences across years
Interaction effects between team and year
The dataset contains detailed information on Formula 1 pit stops, including:

Pit stop duration (response variable)
Team (Car)
Year

To ensure consistency, only teams and years with sufficient participation were included in the analysis.
Key Features
Implementation of two-way ANOVA
Analysis of main effects and interaction effects
Interpretation of statistical significance
Data visualization to support findings
Use of course-based methods and lecture code only
Results Summary
Pit stop times showed variation across different teams
Differences across years were analyzed to observe performance trends
Interaction effects helped determine whether team performance changed over time
Future Improvements
Include more seasons and teams for broader analysis
Apply post-hoc tests (e.g., Tukey HSD) for deeper comparisons

Project Purpose

This project demonstrates:

Application of ANOVA in real-world data
